Autonomous Drone Swarm Navigation and Multitarget Tracking With Island Policy-Based Optimization Framework
Suleman Qamar, Saddam Hussain Khan, Muhammad Arif Arshad, Maryam Qamar, Jeonghwan Gwak, Asifullah Khan*,
IEEE Acess, 2022/08/26
Paper Link

This study introduces an autonomous approach utilizing deep reinforcement learning for swarm navigation in complex 3D environments with static and dynamic obstacles, as well as resistive forces like linear drag, angular drag, and gravity. A novel island policy optimization model is presented to simultaneously track multiple dynamic targets, enhancing swarm dynamics. New reward functions are devised for robust swarm formation and target tracking. The strategy comprises four main components: 1) Island policy-based optimization for multiple targets tracking, 2) Novel reward functions for dynamic target tracking, 3) Improved policy and critic-based framework for dynamic swarm management, and 4) Memory. The results demonstrate that the proposed strategy improves swarm navigation, achieving high cumulative reward and low policy loss. Simulations indicate the framework's efficiency in tracking multiple static and dynamic targets in complex environments.
Drone Navigation Using Region and Edge Exploitation-Based Deep CNN
Muhammad Arif Arshad, Saddam Hussain Khan, Suleman Qamar, Muhammad Waleed Khan, Iqbal Murtaza, Jeonghwan Gwak, Asifullah Khan*,
IEEE Acess, 2022/09/06
Paper Link

The proposed research introduces a novel deep Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) called Drone Split-Transform-and-Merge Region-and-Edge (Drone-STM-RENet) for autonomous drone navigation in complex and dynamic environments. Drones face challenges in navigating independently due to irregular morphology, occlusion, and contrast variations of objects in their surroundings. The CNN employs convolutional blocks to systematically perform region and edge operations, addressing these challenges at multiple levels. Average and max-pooling operations within each block manage region homogeneity and edge properties. The merged convolutional blocks learn texture variation, aiding in effective target discrimination and obstacle avoidance. Drone-STM-RENet generates steering angles and collision probabilities, allowing the drone to navigate, identify risky situations, and respond promptly. Validation on urban road datasets demonstrates high performance, suggesting the model's generalizability for real-time autonomous drone navigation in diverse environments. Key metrics include explained variance (0.99), recall (95.47%), accuracy (96.26%), and F-score (91.95%).
Saliency Prediction in Uncategorized Videos Based on Audio-Visual Correlation
Maryam Qamar, Suleman Qamar*, Muhammad Muneeb, Sung-Ho Bae, Anis Rahman
IEEE Acess, 2023/02/13
Paper Link /

The study focuses on enhancing saliency modeling for intelligent machines to perceive and interpret their surroundings by incorporating audio information into traditional spatio-temporal saliency models. The proposed audiovisual saliency model synchronizes low-level audio and visual features to create a comprehensive saliency map. Evaluation against eye fixations data from the DIEM video dataset demonstrates that the model outperforms two state-of-the-art visual spatio-temporal saliency models. This outcome supports the hypothesis that an audiovisual model performs better than a purely visual model, particularly for natural, uncategorized videos.
AI Credit: Machine Learning Based Credit Score Analysis
Suleman Qamar, Muhammad Hanif Durad , Faizan Ul Islam , Syeda Rozeena Saleha , Muhammad Hamza , Allay Hyder Urooj , Syed Muhammad Abrar Akber ,
Journal of Computing & Biomedical Informatics, 2023/06/05
Paper Link

The study explores the efficacy of various machine learning classifiers for credit scoring and bankruptcy prediction, with a focus on mitigating the risk of financial crisis. The system aims to assist both credit applicants and creditors in assessing eligibility and potential credit amounts, respectively. Using the "GIVE ME SOME CREDIT" dataset from Kaggle, applicants were categorized based on timely bill payments. Machine learning classifiers such as bagging, logistic regression, gradient classifier, and random forest classifier were employed, with XGboost demonstrating superior performance, achieving 94% accuracy, an ROC of 86%, and a 92% F1 score. The study emphasizes the potential of machine learning in reducing the risk of financial instability and aiding in informed credit decisions.
Autonomous Drone Swarm Navigation in Complex Environments
Suleman Qamar*, Maryam Qamar, Muhammad Shahbaz, Muhammad Arif Arshad, Najmus Saher Shah, Asifullah Khan
2022 19th International Bhurban Conference on Applied Sciences and Technology (IBCAST), 2022/068/16
Paper Link

This paper focuses on swarm intelligence, where a group of drones exhibits learned behavior to perform various tasks like complex environment navigation, obstacle avoidance, and single-target tracking. Traditional manual formulation of artificial swarm systems has limitations in adapting to changing environments. To address this, an Autonomous Drone Swarm Navigation (ADSN) system is introduced, utilizing a customized architecture based on Truly Proximal Policy Optimization (TPPO) with memory cells. The system is tested in 3D environments designed for conductive learning, with measures like Mean Cumulative Reward (MCR), Value Loss (VL), and Entropy used to evaluate performance. The results show that incorporating memory enhances model performance, with TPPO consistently outperforming other models in terms of convergence speed and stability. The proposed model, applicable in maze navigation, target tracking, and real-time scenarios, demonstrates significant improvements over existing methodologies. Additionally, Soft Actor Critic (SAC) is noted to be highly dependent on hyperparameter values, leading to varying results.
Autonomous Drone Navigation using Deep Convolutional Neural Network
Muhammad Arif Arshad*, Saddam Hussain Khan, Muhammad Waleed Khan, Suleman Qamar, Asifullah Khan,
Seventh International Conference on Aerospace Science and Engineering (ICASE 2021), 2021/12/01
Paper Link

The research paper addresses the challenges faced by drones navigating independently in dynamic environments through an information-driven strategy. A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) is proposed to safely pilot a drone through city streets, providing steering angles for obstacle avoidance and collision probability for timely responses. Data for training the UAV is collected using cameras mounted on cars and bikes in urban areas. Despite being trained on urban roads, the proposed architecture exhibits a highly generalized navigation strategy with impressive performance metrics, including a high recall of 99.38%, great accuracy of 97.78%, and a good F-score of 94.81%. The results suggest that the proposed technique, surpassing previous approaches, is suitable for autonomous drone navigation, showcasing superior accuracy, recall, F-score, and precision.
